## What this sprint added

The second batch of **per-workflow SEO signature libraries** — the elite playbooks for the three content/authority workflows, authored in the `bober.seo-*` signature-block format defined in Sprint 2 and grounded in the `research-20260715-ultimate-seo-agents-skills-research.md` evidence base. Three data files landed (all pure markdown, no code behavior): `skills/bober.seo-content-decay/SKILL.md` (**7 blocks**, NavBoost decay-as-failed-clicks + the post-HCU decay baseline), `skills/bober.seo-topical-map/SKILL.md` (**7 blocks**, `siteFocusScore`/`siteRadius` topical authority + AI Overview query fan-out coverage), and `skills/bober.seo-rank-track/SKILL.md` (**7 blocks**, AIO-citation-vs-rank decoupling + zero-click reality). All **21 blocks parse valid, zero dropped**; every block carries a `PrimarySourceUrl` that was `curl`-verified HTTP-200 (6 unique URLs, verified pre and post authoring), and no block cites a claim the research report REFUTED (ChatGPT-Wikipedia dominance, Reddit-leads-AI-Overviews). No parser/index/retriever/type change — the `SeoPlaybookIndex` auto-discovers these three new skills by directory glob, so they light up with zero code change. No analyzer, adapter, CLI, or hub emitter yet (later sprints).

## Public surface

These are **data files**, not code symbols — they are consumed by `SeoPlaybookParser.parse()` (Sprint 2) and auto-discovered by `SeoPlaybookIndex`. The three new libraries and their key signatures:

- `skills/bober.seo-content-decay/SKILL.md` — 7 signatures, **6 auto-safe + 1 human-approve**. The mechanism block `navboost-decay-expected-clicks` (cited to `ipullrank.com/google-algo-leak`) encodes the leak's framing of decay as a *failure to keep driving a page's expected click share* over NavBoost's rolling 13-month window — not raw rank alone; `navboost-13month-refresh-window` sets refresh cadence inside that window; `content-decay-hcu-baseline` (cited to `detailed.com/q3/`) encodes Detailed's Q3 study of 522 content-first sites where 64% lost traffic (median -471K visits / -17.8%); plus `goodclicks-badclicks-early-warning`, `refresh-cadence-monitoring`, `content-refresh-genuine-effort` (guard against low-effort scaled regeneration / the `contentEffort` signal). `large-scale-rewrite-consolidation-approval` is `human-approve` because mass rewrite/consolidation/redirect of decayed pages mutates the live site.
- `skills/bober.seo-topical-map/SKILL.md` — 7 signatures, **6 auto-safe + 1 human-approve**. `sitefocusscore-topical-dedication` and `siteradius-deviation-outlier-map` / `new-node-central-theme-alignment` encode the leak's `siteFocusScore` (site-wide dedication to one topic) and `siteRadius` (per-page deviation from the central theme, high-deviation = documented ranking outlier), cited to the SparkToro leak disclosure; `query-fan-out-coverage-map` (cited to `ahrefs.com/blog/ai-overview-citations-top-10/`) says map the AI Overview query fan-out set of sub-questions, not just the head keyword; `topical-authority-cluster-completeness` treats cluster completeness as mechanically scored; `commercial-intent-map-priority` (graded `verified`, cited to the Semrush AIO study) reprioritizes toward the commercial/transactional AIO intent shift. `map-buildout-at-scale-approval` is `human-approve` because bulk-publishing new nodes at scale is a policy-exposed structural change.
- `skills/bober.seo-rank-track/SKILL.md` — 7 signatures, **all auto-safe** (every tactic is pure read-only monitoring/analysis). `aio-citation-rank-decouple` (graded `verified`, cited to Ahrefs) tracks AIO-citation coverage as a metric *separate* from organic rank — only **38%** of AIO-cited pages rank top-10 as of Jan 2026 (down from ~76% in Jul 2025); `zero-click-reality-check` (cited to Semrush) records that the zero-click rate slightly *decreased* (33.75% → 31.53%) after AIOs appeared, against the click-collapse narrative; `aio-prevalence-volatility-cadence` (6.49% → 24.61% → 15.69% across 2025) and `rank-track-monitoring-cadence` argue for standing recurring re-measurement; `commercial-intent-trigger-shift` tracks the AIO money-query intent shift; `ghost-citation-two-metric-tracking` (cited to the Semrush/Kevin Indig ghost-citations study, 61.7% linked-but-unnamed) splits citation-rate from mention-rate; `navboost-click-quality-leading-indicator` watches good/bad-click proxies as a leading rank indicator.

## How to use / how it fits

Nothing new runs on its own — these files are the retrieval corpus for the analyzer sprint (10). `SeoPlaybookIndex.load()` already `readdir`s every `skills/bober.seo-*/SKILL.md`, so these three appear in the flat signature list and in `SeoPlaybookRetriever.retrieve({ workflow })` results the moment they exist on disk. A `retrieve({ workflow: "content-decay" })` (or `"topical-map"` / `"rank-track"`) call now returns these 7 signatures ranked ahead of the generic floor (appended, deduped by `playbookId`). Retrieval is workflow-tagged: every block declares its `Workflows:` line matching the skill's workflow, which the extended `skills-content.test.ts` asserts per-file.

## Notes for maintainers

- **Citation discipline is the load-bearing invariant here, and it is enforced by the evaluator, not the parser.** The parser only checks a `PrimarySourceUrl` is present and non-empty — it cannot tell a real slug from a plausible hallucinated one (this is exactly what burned Sprint 2's iteration 1). All 6 unique URLs across these three files were taken verbatim from the verified map and re-`curl`ed to 200 both before and after authoring. If you edit a citation, `curl -sI` it for a `200` first.
- **`policyClass` discipline: auto-safe vs human-approve.** The split is not cosmetic — auto-safe blocks are read-only detection/monitoring/analysis an analyzer may act on directly; the two `human-approve` blocks (`large-scale-rewrite-consolidation-approval`, `map-buildout-at-scale-approval`) gate the only tactics that mutate a live site (mass rewrite/consolidation/redirect, bulk publishing at scale). `rank-track` is deliberately **all auto-safe** — it is pure monitoring with no site-mutating tactic. No `never-encode` tactic appears, and no site-mutating tactic is tagged `auto-safe`. Do not downgrade a `human-approve` block without the same human-approval reasoning in the invariant.
- **No REFUTED claim is cited.** These three workflows are exactly where the panicky misconceptions live (AIO-drives-zero-click-collapse; citation == rank; citation == brand named). The blocks deliberately encode the *measured* counter-facts — zero-click slightly decreased, 38% citation/top-10 decouple, 61.7% ghost citations — rather than the refuted narratives.
- **Evidence grades:** 7 of the 21 blocks are graded `verified` (the Ahrefs 38% study, the Semrush AIO-prevalence/intent/zero-click study, the ghost-citations study — 6 in `rank-track`, 1 `commercial-intent-map-priority` in `topical-map`). The remaining 14 are `single-source`, mostly the single Content Warehouse leak analyses (ipullrank.com / sparktoro.com) and Detailed's Q3 baseline; the leak-derived signals are inherently single-source, treat them as such.
- **These skill files and `SeoPlaybookParser` are one executable spec.** The block format lives in `skills/bober.seo-generic/SKILL.md` ("Signature Block Format"); a format change must be mirrored in the parser and vice versa. Each of these three files points back to it in its header.
