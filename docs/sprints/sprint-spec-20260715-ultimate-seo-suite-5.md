Nothing new runs on its own — these files are the retrieval corpus for the analyzer sprint (10). `SeoPlaybookIndex.load()` already `readdir`s every `skills/bober.seo-*/SKILL.md`, so these three appear in the flat signature list and in `SeoPlaybookRetriever.retrieve({ workflow })` results the moment they exist on disk: `retrieve({ workflow: "ai-visibility" })` and `retrieve({ workflow: "parasite-watch" })` now return their workflow-tagged signatures ranked ahead of the generic floor. ## Notes for maintainers

- **The safety model is the point of this sprint, and it is enforced two ways.** (1) The parser **structurally** drops any `PolicyClass: never-encode` block at `src/seo/parser.ts:111`, so a banned tactic can never reach an analyzer prompt — this is why all 5 adversarial tactics are authored as `never-encode` boundary blocks (documented for human readers, invisible to the retriever). (2) The new `FORBIDDEN_ACTION_PATTERNS` guard in `src/seo/skills-content.test.ts` is a **belt-and-suspenders** empirical check: `assertNoForbiddenActions()` runs a set of regexes (place-on-parasite/high-DR-host, buy links, register expired domain, generate mass pages, poison) against every *surviving* signature's `tactic` and asserts `policyClass !== "never-encode"`. It caught nothing to fix (the authoring was already safe) but proves the guarantee against the real asset rather than assuming it.
- **Detection ≠ tactic.** The parasite-watch and iGaming detection blocks read `auto-safe` because they are pure read-only SERP monitoring of a **competitor**; the phrasing is deliberately Detect/Monitor/Flag and every one says so. Do not reword any of them into an instruction to *perform* placement — that would either trip `FORBIDDEN_ACTION_PATTERNS` or (worse, if the regex misses it) leak a banned tactic. When in doubt, author it as a `never-encode` boundary block.
- **VERIFIED vs PERISHABLE in ai-visibility.** The correlation figures (0.737 YouTube, 0.66–0.71 branded mentions ≫ 0.218 backlinks, 61.7% ghost citations, 38% AIO/top-10 decouple) are graded `verified` and cited to their study. The **per-platform citation-mix** figures are `single-source` and tagged `[PERISHABLE, dated 2026]` — platform algorithms churn fast; treat those specific percentages as dated-as-of-measurement and re-measure on a cadence. A generator self-caught and fixed a Reddit-"dominates" **REFUTED** false-positive during authoring; no REFUTED claim (ChatGPT-Wikipedia dominance, Reddit-leads-AI-Overviews) is cited.
- **Citation discipline (enforced by the evaluator, not the parser).** The parser only checks a `PrimarySourceUrl` is present; it cannot tell a real slug from a hallucinated one (the lesson that burned Sprint 2). All **11 unique citation URLs** across these three files were `curl`-verified HTTP-200 — including the **3 Google spam-policy URLs** used in the `never-encode` boundary blocks (`.../essentials/spam-policies`, `.../2024/11/site-reputation-abuse`, `.../2024/03/core-update-spam-policies`), spot-checked against `developers.google.com` before encoding per sc-5-4. If you edit a citation, `curl -sI` it for a `200` first.
- **This completes the knowledge library.** With Sprints 2–5 the retrieval corpus is done: `bober.seo-generic` (floor) + the 8 workflow libraries + `bober.seo-verticals`. The later sprints (analyzer 10, adapters, CLI, hub emitter) consume this corpus — they do not add to it. No detection **logic** exists in code yet; per the sprint non-goal this is knowledge-only.
